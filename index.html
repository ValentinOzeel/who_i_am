PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCES

  • R&D

    PhD

    -- Sep 2019 - Dec 2022 --

    Structural and functional analysis of physicochemical interactions of the hemagglutinin of influenza A viruses : evolutionary constraints and perspectives for the development of broad-spectrum therapeutic antivirals and universal vaccines

    - Computation of interatomic physico-chemical interactions and their distribution (intra-/inter-protomer and sub-units) based on 281 hemagglutinin (HA) 3D structures, accross influenza A virus classifications.
    - Computation of highly conserved physico-chemical interactions accross classifications.
    - Investigation of complex inter-protomer salt bridges highly conserved across HA clades 1, 3, 9, and 11, conducted through various wet-lab techniques (including mutagenesis, Western blotting, cleavage assays, flow cytometry, fusion assays, live imaging, reverse genetics, and viral kinetics).
    - Demonstrated that conserved interactions are crucial for the viability of H1N1 mutant viruses, which lose their HA fusogenic properties at acidic pH (and thus the ability to infect cells), irrespective of the mutations introduced (with the exception of E->D, which maintains the salt-bridge).


    My doctoral research produced valuable insights into potential therapeutic targets resistant to escape mutations in influenza viruses and garnered praise from experts in structural biology and virology.
